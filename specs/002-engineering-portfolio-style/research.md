# Research: Engineering Portfolio Visual Direction

## Decision 1: Typography pairing strategy
- Decision: Use a refined serif for hero/section headings, a readable sans-serif for body/navigation, and sparse mono usage for technical metadata labels.
- Rationale: Delivers premium editorial tone with technical clarity and avoids template-like visual monotony.
- Alternatives considered: Single sans stack only; rejected because it weakens hierarchy and premium feel.

## Decision 2: Palette philosophy and accent discipline
- Decision: Use warm off-white/soft stone backgrounds, near-black primary text, muted gray secondary text, and one restrained accent (deep steel/slate blue or muted brass) applied sparingly.
- Rationale: Keeps visual weight in type and layout while preserving calm confidence.
- Alternatives considered: High-saturation startup blues or purple-heavy palette; rejected because they conflict with style guardrails.

## Decision 3: Layout rhythm and section architecture
- Decision: Favor generous vertical spacing, narrow readable text widths, deliberate section breaks, and asymmetric-but-balanced compositions.
- Rationale: Supports editorial scanning and makes case-study content feel intentional rather than card-grid boilerplate.
- Alternatives considered: Dense card-heavy dashboard style; rejected due to clutter and equal-weight hierarchy problems.

## Decision 4: Surface and border treatment
- Decision: Use sleeker low-contrast surfaces, soft borders, and minimal elevation with selective contrast for key interactive affordances.
- Rationale: Achieves modern technical polish without visual noise or glassmorphism overload.
- Alternatives considered: Heavy shadows and frosted overlays; rejected for being trend-heavy and distracting.

## Decision 5: Motion behavior profile
- Decision: Use restrained fade/slide reveals, gentle hover feedback, and short transitions focused on orientation, not spectacle.
- Rationale: Reinforces modern polish while preserving reading flow and performance.
- Alternatives considered: Scroll-jacking and highly animated effects; rejected by explicit guardrails.

## Decision 6: Imagery and framing
- Decision: Present screenshots/visuals with clean framing and breathing room; ensure missing visuals degrade gracefully to text-first credibility.
- Rationale: Keeps credibility high and avoids empty decorative placeholders.
- Alternatives considered: Decorative image-heavy hero treatment; rejected due to reduced signal density.
